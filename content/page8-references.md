Primary Research Source
The analysis and data summarized on this website are based on the following publication:
Chinoy, S., Nunn, N., Sequeira, S., & Stantcheva, S. (2025). Zero-sum thinking and the roots of U.S. political differences (NBER Working Paper No. 31688; revised April 2025). National Bureau of Economic Research. https://doi.org/10.3386/w31688

Foundations: Cooperation, Competition, and Zero-Sum Thinking
These works laid the conceptual foundations for understanding zero-sum and non–zero-sum perspectives in social interaction.
Deutsch, M. (1949). A theory of cooperation and competition. Human Relations, 2(2), 129–152. https://doi.org/10.1177/001872674900200204
Deutsch, M. (1973). The resolution of conflict: Constructive and destructive processes. Yale University Press.
________________________________________
Zero-Sum Thinking in Social Perception and Group Relations
These studies examine how zero-sum beliefs shape perceptions of intergroup relations, fairness, and threat.
Norton, M. I., & Sommers, S. R. (2011). Whites see racism as a zero-sum game that they are now losing. Perspectives on Psychological Science, 6(3), 215–218. https://doi.org/10.1177/1745691611406922
________________________________________
Political Attitudes, Ideology, and Polarization
This literature links zero-sum thinking to political ideology, polarization, and policy attitudes.
Davidai, S., & Ongis, M. (2019). The politics of zero-sum thinking: The relationship between political ideology and the belief that life is a zero-sum game. Science Advances, 5(11), eaay3761. https://doi.org/10.1126/sciadv.aay3761
________________________________________
Measurement and Psychological Foundations
These studies develop and validate measurement approaches and examine the psychological mechanisms underlying zero-sum thinking.
Andrews Fearon, P., & Götz, F. M. (2024). The zero-sum mindset. Journal of Personality and Social Psychology, 127(4), 758–795. https://doi.org/10.1037/pspa0000404
Molnar, A. C., & Sloman, S. (2026). Zero-sum bias in politicized problem solving. Cognition, 270, 106408. https://doi.org/10.1016/j.cognition.2025.106408
________________________________________
Reviews and Synthesis of the Literature
This review provides an overview of how zero-sum thinking has been studied across disciplines in recent years.
Matsunaga, L. H., Petersen, J., Aoki, T., & Faiad, C. (2025). The science of zero-sum thinking: A scoping review of ten years of empirical research. Dynamics of Asymmetric Conflict. Advance online publication. https://doi.org/10.1080/17467586.2024.2441504

Data Access and Transparency
This website presents aggregated summaries only, derived from the published research listed above. It does not host or distribute individual-level survey data.
Readers interested in replication materials, full survey instruments, and technical appendices should consult the links provided by the original authors in the primary publication.
